Research

My research focuses on using microeconomics and rich historical data to learn about socioeconomic processes such as migrant assimilation and technological change, as well as improving the empirical methods commonly used in economic history.

Currently, I am studying the effect of family structure on migrant assimilation in the early twentieth century. I am also working on quantifying and correcting the bias that occurs in estimates obtained from probabilistically linked datasets with Lucas Mation. I'm also assisting a research team consisting of Richard Hornbeck, Anders Humlum, Martin Rotemberg and other super smart people, where we are currently linking large sets of historical manufacturing data using machine learning.
